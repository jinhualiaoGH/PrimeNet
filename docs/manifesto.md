# PrimeNet Manifesto

## Building an Observatory for Prime Information Structures

---

> "Observe first. Explain later."

PrimeNet was founded on a simple scientific conviction:

Many important discoveries begin not with theory, but with careful observation.

For centuries, the distribution of prime numbers has inspired deep mathematical questions. While numerous theoretical advances have been made, the rapidly increasing availability of computational resources now makes possible a complementary scientific approach: systematic, large-scale observation.

PrimeNet embraces this opportunity.

Rather than beginning with specific conjectures, PrimeNet builds an observational infrastructure capable of collecting, measuring, preserving, and organizing arithmetic information on an unprecedented scale.

The objective is not to replace mathematical proof.

The objective is to expand mathematical observation.

---

# The Observatory Philosophy

PrimeNet adopts the philosophy of a scientific observatory.

Modern astronomy did not begin by explaining the universe.

It first built telescopes.

It developed instruments.

It accumulated observations.

It constructed catalogs.

Only then did deeper theories emerge.

PrimeNet follows the same philosophy for computational number theory.

Instead of observing galaxies, PrimeNet observes arithmetic.

Instead of measuring photons, PrimeNet measures information.

Instead of building sky surveys, PrimeNet builds information atlases.

---

# Scientific Principles

PrimeNet is guided by several principles.

## Observation before explanation

Reliable observations should precede theoretical interpretation whenever possible.

---

## Reproducibility

Every published result should be reproducible from archived datasets and documented computational procedures.

---

## Standardized Instruments

Scientific measurements should be performed using reusable computational instruments rather than one-time scripts.

---

## Data Preservation

Large computational observations represent valuable scientific resources.

PrimeNet treats datasets as permanent scientific assets.

---

## Open Science

Scientific progress benefits from transparent methods, documented workflows, and openly accessible computational results.

---

## Theory Neutrality

PrimeNet is not designed to support any particular mathematical hypothesis.

Its purpose is to provide reliable observational evidence that may assist future theoretical development.

---

# The PrimeNet Observatory

PrimeNet consists of multiple complementary observatories.

• Prime Repository

• Event Observatory

• Information Observatory

• Transition Observatory

• Geometry Observatory

• Taxonomy Observatory

• Invariant Observatory

Each observatory investigates a different aspect of arithmetic information.

Together they form an integrated scientific infrastructure.

---

# Long-Term Vision

PrimeNet is intended to become a long-term scientific platform.

As computational resources increase, the repository will continue to expand.

New observational instruments will be developed.

New atlases will be constructed.

New invariants may be discovered.

Future researchers should be able to build upon these observations just as astronomers build upon previous sky surveys.

PrimeNet is designed not as a single project, but as a growing scientific observatory.
