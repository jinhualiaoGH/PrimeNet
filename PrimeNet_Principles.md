# PrimeNet Principles

Version: 1.0

---

# PrimeNet Principles

PrimeNet is founded on the belief that arithmetic may be studied through
systematic, reproducible observation.

These principles define the long-term philosophy of the project and guide
its architecture, software, repositories, observatories, and scientific
practice.

Unlike implementation details, these principles are intended to remain
stable over time.

---

# Principle 1

## The Prime Index Is the Canonical Coordinate System

Prime indices define position within prime space.

Prime values are measurements attached to those coordinates.

Every PrimeNet repository and observatory uses the prime index as its
primary coordinate system.

---

# Principle 2

## Observe Before Interpreting

PrimeNet records measurable arithmetic phenomena.

Interpretation belongs to scientific analysis rather than canonical
observations.

Measurements should remain objective, reproducible, and independent of
hypotheses.

---

# Principle 3

## Measure Before Theorizing

Reliable scientific theories begin with reliable observations.

PrimeNet emphasizes accurate measurement before mathematical
interpretation.

---

# Principle 4

## Reproducibility Is Fundamental

Every observation should be independently reproducible.

PrimeNet records sufficient metadata to reproduce every published
measurement.

Scientific credibility depends upon reproducibility.

---

# Principle 5

## Scientific Infrastructure Enables Discovery

PrimeNet does not attempt to prove mathematical conjectures.

Instead, it builds the infrastructure through which new observations,
patterns, and hypotheses may emerge.

Infrastructure precedes discovery.

---

# Principle 6

## Observations Are Permanent Scientific Artifacts

Observations are not temporary program output.

They are scientific records that should remain reproducible,
verifiable, searchable, and reusable.

---

# Principle 7

## Separate Data, Measurement, and Interpretation

PrimeNet distinguishes three distinct layers.

Arithmetic data

↓

Scientific observations

↓

Scientific interpretation

Each layer serves a different purpose and should remain independent.

---

# Principle 8

## Canonical Representations Reduce Ambiguity

PrimeNet prefers one canonical representation over multiple equivalent
representations whenever practical.

Examples include

• Prime indices

• Left-owned gap repositories

• Canonical observation documents

Canonical representations simplify scientific communication.

---

# Principle 9

## Build Once, Observe Many Times

The repository should be constructed once and reused for many different
scientific investigations.

Observation should become significantly less expensive than repository
construction.

---

# Principle 10

## Scientific Software Should Be Modular

Repositories

Observatories

Validation

Serialization

Publication

Each component should evolve independently while remaining compatible
through well-defined interfaces.

---

# Principle 11

## Validation Is Part of Measurement

Measurements are incomplete without validation.

Every repository and every observation should verify its own integrity.

Validation is a scientific requirement rather than an implementation
detail.

---

# Principle 12

## Scale Should Not Change Meaning

PrimeNet seeks scientific measurements that remain meaningful across
multiple numerical scales.

Cross-scale consistency is a fundamental objective of the observatory.

---

# Principle 13

## Computational Observation Complements Mathematical Theory

PrimeNet does not replace mathematical proof.

It complements traditional mathematics by providing systematic,
large-scale observational evidence.

Observation and proof are different but mutually supportive forms of
scientific inquiry.

---

# Principle 14

## Scientific Knowledge Should Accumulate

Every repository

Every observation

Every validation

Every publication

should contribute to a growing, reproducible body of scientific
knowledge.

PrimeNet is designed as a long-term scientific observatory rather than
a collection of isolated computational experiments.

---

The objective is not merely to compute prime numbers.

The objective is to establish a reproducible computational observatory
through which prime information structures may be systematically
observed, measured, validated, preserved, and explored.

PrimeNet exists to provide scientific infrastructure upon which future
discoveries may be built.

---

## Closing Statement

PrimeNet is designed to outlive any individual implementation,
publication, or investigation.

Its principles provide a stable foundation upon which future software,
repositories, observatories, and scientific discoveries may be built.

These principles should evolve only with great care, preserving the
continuity, reproducibility, and scientific integrity of the PrimeNet
project.

# Principle 15

## Computational Infrastructure Is a Scientific Instrument

PrimeNet treats software, repositories, and observatories as scientific
instruments.

The quality of scientific observations depends upon the quality,
stability, validation, and reproducibility of the computational
infrastructure.

Software engineering is therefore an integral part of scientific
measurement rather than merely a supporting activity.

# Principle 16

## Simplicity Enables Longevity

PrimeNet favors simple, canonical, and well-defined designs over
unnecessary complexity.

Algorithms, repositories, interfaces, and observatories should be
understandable, maintainable, and reproducible.

Long-term scientific infrastructure depends upon architectural clarity
rather than implementation complexity.
