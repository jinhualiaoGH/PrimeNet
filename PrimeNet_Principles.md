# PrimeNet

PrimeNet is a reproducible observatory of arithmetic spaces.

Its purpose is to preserve, measure, and explore arithmetic information through deterministic transformations.

---

# Foundational Principles

## 1. Arithmetic Spaces

Arithmetic is studied through a hierarchy of observable spaces.

---

## 2. Deterministic Mappings

Every derived space is generated by deterministic transformation.

Identical inputs always produce identical outputs.

---

## 3. Immutable Foundation

Prime Space (P0) is immutable.

Once certified, it is never modified.

---

## 4. Everything Else is Derived

Every repository layer, observatory, atlas, and scientific product is derived from previously certified spaces.

---

## 5. Preserve Information

Store information.

Do not store temporary computation.

---

## 6. CPU Time is Renewable

CPU time is renewable.

Storage is permanent.

Optimize permanent storage before temporary computation.

---

## 7. Ideas are Permanent

Ideas are permanent.

Architecture is long-lived.

Code is renewable.

Implementations evolve.

Principles endure.

---

## 8. Reproducibility

Every PrimeNet result must be

- deterministic
- reproducible
- verifiable

---

## 9. Observe Before Explaining

PrimeNet begins with observation.

Understanding emerges from reproducible evidence.

Observation precedes interpretation.

---

## 10. Simplicity

Simple structures are preferred.

Simple architectures are easier to verify.

Simple ideas are easier to preserve.

---
